# ⚠️ Problem

Escape rooms are hard to run consistently. Most teams piece together docs, chats, folders, and spreadsheets. It works until the day it doesn’t.

### The day-to-day problems

#### Room content ends up scattered

Puzzles, hints, and media live in different places. Updates are easy to miss. New staff learns “how we do it” by trial and error.

#### Running sessions isn’t repeatable

Game masters improvise tooling during live runs. That slows down hint delivery and increases mistakes. It also makes training harder.

#### Rich media delivery is awkward

Images, audio, and video clues often require separate tools. That adds friction during the game.

#### There’s no real feedback loop

Owners want data-driven decisions. But many rooms only track “win/loss” or a rough time. Full logs of actions are rare, so improvements are guesswork.

### What this leads to

* Slower onboarding for new game masters.
* Inconsistent player experience between staff.
* Harder post-game analysis for owners.
* Fewer improvements shipped over time.
